Zero Waste Movement


Clearing out space and living a clutter free minimalistic life is great! But as we toss things out, there is a chance we might toss few things that would have been better recycled, composted or donated.
Getting rid of waste properly might be the next step, since we dont want our decluttering to become wastefulness.
What is Zero Waste Movement? Followers of the Zero Waste Movement abide by the Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, and Rot mantra. It is about reducing our impact on the health of the planet by reducing the waste they produce. Learn more about how to Declutter waste free here: The Zero Waste Movement.

Mental Clutter


Ever feel like your brain is on overload? Just as we declutter our physical space, our minds too need tidying up from time to time. When we get rid of our mental baggage, we are able to think more clearly and stay focused.
Journaling is one of many great mind decluttering techniques anybody can do. "Writing a journal allows you to manage anxiety and cope with depression, as it’s a healthy outlet to release bottled emotions," - states a University of Rochester Medical Center report.
Learn more ways to declutter your mind and free up some mental space: How to Declutter Your Mind

Considering Indoor Plants?


Living in a cluttered space can make you feel stressed, but have you considered getting an indoor plant? Plants are not just for home decoration, but they interact with your body, mind and home in ways that enhance the quality of life. Studies have also proven that indoor plants improve concentration and productivity by up to 15 percent! They reduce stress levels and boost your mood. This is why it’s a great idea to keep potted plants where you work as it will reduce stress and anxiety leading to better productivity.
And If you have one, here is a guide on choosing the best plant for your room: Home Plant Guide

Organizing Clothes


The hardest part of decluttering the closet can be not knowing how to get started. Most people have multiple items in their closets that they rarely, or even never wear. But looking around at all the piles of clothing can leave a feeling of embarrasment and guilt, and that's a no-no, because it just makes you feel bad.
There are many methods to help with decluttering closets. The KonMari method is a system of simplifying and organizing your closet by getting rid of clothes that do not bring joy into your life. The positive thing to do is to let them go. But “letting go” doesn’t mean you can’t store or donate items! Here is the ultimate KonMari guide: The Ultimate KonMari Guide. You can also find KonMari's books here: konmari.com
